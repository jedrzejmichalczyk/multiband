Multiband Zolotarev filter synthesis

Browser-side implementation of Lunot, Seyfert, Bila & Nasser (2008) “Certified Computation of Optimal Multiband Filtering Functions.” The solver is ~800 lines of C++ compiled to WebAssembly; the LP back-end is HiGHS.

Gallery

Pre-computed results from the Python reference implementation — these are the paper's two design examples, reproduced with the asymmetric return-loss / rejection specifications. Use these as a starting point, or load them directly into the editor below.

Example 1 — 9-3 dual-band

Passbands
[−1, −0.625], [0.25, 1]
Stopbands
[−10, −1.188], [−0.5, 0.125], [1.212, 10]
Specs
RL 20 dB; reject 15 dB outer, 30 dB middle
Result
17 alternation points (≥ 14 needed) ⇒ certified optimal.

Example 2 — 7-3 dual-band

Passbands
[−1, −0.383], [0.383, 1]
Stopbands
piecewise 10 / 15 / 20 / 40 dB targets
Specs
RL 23 dB
Result
12 alternation points (= 12 target) ⇒ certified optimal.
